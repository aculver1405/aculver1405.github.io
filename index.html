Information on Job Openings

Finding a job in today's society can be a little difficult, however this information will level the playing field and increase your chances.

Networking

Most job vacancies aren't really advertised, so you'll have to find connections some how. That's where networking comes in. You can join professional associations, connect with friends, old mentors, or professional in your field, and use social media to find jobs.

Job Boards, Career Websites, Company Websites, and Job Fairs

There are still job boards in local centers that have postings about vacancies, however everything has moved to a more digital era, meaning that these job boards are now virtual and online. There are tons of sites dedicated to posting job openings. I will post some of those sites below. Job Fairs are usually held by specific industries. When finding and going to a job fair, always have multiple resumes with you and present yourself professionally.

Temping and Internships

Temping, short for Temporary Employment, can lead to full time employment. You will get all the experience you need for that position and be a top candidate when a job for that specific company is available. Internships on the other hand, pretty much has the same kind of effect. Volunteering your time for free to get great professional experience puts you in the same boat.

    • Resume & Cover Letter

    A good cover letter and resume is essential to getting the attention of future employers. It should clearly highlight your accomplishments and experience as well as form a connection between the the postion you are applying for.

    Cover Letter

    Your cover letter may be the make or break between whether your resume gets view or thrown to the side. When writing your cover letter, you want to clearly express what the company needs what you can offer. Take time to highlight how your skills and achievements will be of use to that company.

    Resumes

    The purpose of a resume is to market you to employers. It needs to demonstrate that you are employable.

  • How you have met the job and the organisation's requirements
  • That you have the right qualifications and education
  • That you have the right experience and skills
  • That you have the right level of professionalism for the job

    • This is the key to getting the interview. Your resume should include information on your education, history, and skill set. When choosing the information, make sure that it is related to the postion. Why would the particular complany be interested in you? It's ok to maximize what you've done, just make sure it's not a lie.

    Filling out a Job Application

    Be prepared beforehand because you might have to fill out the application on the spot.

    Read the instructions carefully (It might ask for a specific pen color). Neatly write out everything and re-read it to make sure your grammar is correct.

    Answer all questions clearly and concisely.

    Big Points

    Research your organization - What's their mission? What is their selling points?

    Have some talking points so that you seem interesting and that the conversation never goes awkardly silent.

    Prepare and get your answers ready for the commonly asked interview questions. Also, prepare your own questions as well.

    Have some passion and come off as interesting!

    • Helpful Tips

    Here is a short list of some tips that are extremely helpful in interviews

    Always arrive at least fifteen minutes early

    Business casual, suits, etc. (No jeans)

    When getting a question that you didnt hear correctly or did not understand, ask for clarification.

    Do not beg or sound needy

    Do not act super laid back and casual. This isn't the playground

    Send a thank you note or email as soon as the interview is over.

    Good Luck!
